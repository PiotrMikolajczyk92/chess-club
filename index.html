Why is playing chess so beneficial?

Connecting people

Chess is one of the oldest games in the world, dating back more than 1500 years. As it spread around the world, chess evolved into the game we play today. Through this journey, people from different cultures, ages and backgrounds have come together with a common bond and passion for the game.

Improve creativity

It is said that a person's personality is revealed in his game of chess. A shy and passive person may play more conservatively, while a sociable and open person may be more daring in attack. The great thing about chess is that there is room for all styles and personalities. On the chessboard, everyone in their own way can show creativity by making different moves, devising plans and applying different tactical motives.

Boost problem-solving skills

In each game of chess, challenges and problems await us that must be solved in order to play the best game. Chess helps you think ahead, take your time with decisions, and weigh the pros and cons of your choices. Just like in chess, also in everyday life we try to make the best choices so that they bring positive results in our lives.

Help to focus

As Bobby Fischer said, "Chess requires total concentration." Each chess player can play like a grandmaster for 30 moves, and then in the 31st move he will be distracted and make an elementary mistake that will lose the game! Such intense concentration is also useful in everyday life, when you have to face household chores, daily duties and chasing deadlines.
